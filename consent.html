Study Information

Please read the following information carefully before deciding whether to participate in this study.

Participation is voluntary.
It is your choice whether or not to participate in this research. If you choose to participate, you may change your mind and leave the study at any time. Refusal to participate or stopping your participation will involve no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. If at any time before the completion of the study you close the experiment window or tab, this will be treated as withdrawn consent, however, any data you have provided will not be discarded.
Are there any benefits from being in this research study?
You will receive a visualization reflecting the degree to which you use each of the different psychological principles or dimensions to make assessments of situations. You will also see the average degree to which other participants make use of the same dimensions. You may benefit from knowing that you are contributing to the understanding of human judgment and decision-making.
What are the risks and possible discomforts?
If you choose to participate, we anticipate that you will experience no greater risk or discomfort than you would experience in normal use of a computer for the same period of time.
How will my privacy be protect? What happens to the information you collect?
The data we collect from this study will not be identified by any names, email addresses, or IP addresses. Aggregate data from this study may be presented in scientific publications or talks. Non-identifable data from this study may be shared publicly to further scientific research. Great care will be taken to ensure that it is not possible to collect or recover individual identities from shared data.
Who can I talk to if I have questions?
The researcher for this study is Rebecca Ramnauth, who can be reached at rebecca.ramnauth@yale.edu if you have questions, concerns, or compliants, or if you wish to withdraw from the study.

This study has received ethics approval from the Institutional Review Board at Yale University. They can be reached by phone at (203) 785-4688, or by email at hrpp@yale.edu if your questions, concerns, or complaints are not being answered by the research team, if you want to talk to someone besides the research team, or if you have questions about your rights as a research participant.
What is the purpose of this research?
The purpose of this research is to understand what psychological dimensions people draw upon to make assessments of student performance.
How long will I take part in this research?
As this study in the format of an adaptive survey, your participation will last about 50 trials in the main task which translates to approximately eight minutes. You may continue to perform additional trials at no guaranteed benefit.
What can I expect if I take part in this research?
You will provide basic demographic information and then complete at least 50 trials of the main task. For each trial, you will be shown two student cards showing their attributes and performance on a classroom task. You will rate the qualities of the students on sliding scales from A (More like Student A) to B (More like Student B).
